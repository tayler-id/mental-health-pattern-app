Sound Assets for Mental Health Tracker

This directory should contain the following sound files used by the application. These files are referenced in the useAudioManager.js hook.

click.mp3

A subtle click/tap sound used for UI interactions like button presses. Should be short (50-100ms) and not distracting.

success.mp3

A positive, uplifting sound for successful actions (like submitting a form). Duration around 0.5-1 second.

achievement.mp3

A celebratory sound for unlocking achievements or reaching streak milestones. Should be distinctive and rewarding (1-2 seconds).

error.mp3

A gentle error notification sound that's not alarming but clearly indicates something went wrong. Duration around 0.5 seconds.

streak.mp3

A sound indicating streak progress, slightly different from achievement sound. Duration around 0.5-1 second.

level-up.mp3

A dramatic, exciting sound for major milestones like reaching a new level. Duration around 2 seconds.

notification.mp3

A gentle notification sound for alerts and information messages. Duration around 0.5 seconds.

typing.mp3

A subtle keyboard typing sound for text inputs. Should be very short (50ms) and quiet.

slider-move.mp3

A subtle sound for slider movements. Should be very short (50ms) and not distracting.

ui-sounds.mp3

A sprite sheet containing multiple UI sounds for use with Howler.js sprite functionality.

How to Obtain Sound Files

You have several options for obtaining sound files:

  1. Free Sound Libraries:
    • Freesound.org - A collaborative database of creative-commons licensed sounds
    • Mixkit - Free sound effects for various categories
    • ZapSplat - Free sound effects library
  2. Premium Sound Libraries:
    • SoundSnap - Professional sound effects library
    • Epidemic Sound - High-quality sound effects
  3. Create Your Own:
    • Audacity - Free, open-source audio editing software
    • ChipTone - Browser-based sound effect generator
    • Bfxr - Sound effect generator for chiptunes and simple sounds

When selecting sounds, consider:

  • Keeping sounds subtle and non-intrusive
  • Ensuring they fit together aesthetically
  • Using sounds that are not too jarring or annoying when heard repeatedly
  • Optimizing file sizes to keep the application performant

After obtaining sounds, place them in this directory with the filenames listed above.
